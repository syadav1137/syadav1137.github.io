Research

Feb 2025 – Present
Schumacher Lab
Duke University
Dept. of Biochemistry

Structural Biology and Protein Biochemistry

In the Schumacher Lab, I contribute to the structural and biochemical characterization of bacterial proteins to elucidate their functions at atomic resolution.

My work involves two primary projects: investigating the transcription activation mechanism of DriD, a WYL-domain activator in the noncanonical DNA damage response pathway of Caulobacter crescentus (in press, Science Advances), and characterizing GapR nucleoid-associated proteins — revealing how a phage-encoded GapR forms inhibitory hetero-oligomers with its host homolog to disrupt DNA binding (submitted to Nature Communications). My primary techniques are high-throughput protein purification and Size Exclusion Chromatography (SEC) to isolate stable protein complexes for structural analysis.

May 2023 – Dec 2024
Weintraub Lab
University of Toledo
Dept. of Environmental Sciences

Coastal Biogeochemistry and Interface Dynamics

In the Weintraub Lab, I contributed to the multi-institutional COMPASS-FME project, working with Drs. Roberta Bittencourt-Peixoto and Fausto Machado-Silva to quantify biogeochemical shifts at Terrestrial-Aquatic Interfaces (TAI) — transition zones between land and water that compress and expand with lake and sea level changes.

My work focused on the biogeochemical mechanisms of ghost forest formation, the progressive die-off of upland trees as rising water tables drive soil saturation, triggering methane release and disrupting nutrient cycling. I carried out large-scale field sampling regimes, collecting high-resolution data on CO₂ and CH₄ fluxes using LI-8200 Smart Chambers and monitoring real-time soil physicochemical conditions to track redox potential and oxygen dynamics. Alongside fieldwork, I managed extensive laboratory efforts including porewater extraction via rhizons and lysimeters, total reducing sugar assays, and spectrophotometric analyses of iron and sulfide.

Aug 2022 – May 2023
Gray Lab
University of Toledo
Dept. of Biological Sciences

Plant Molecular Genetics and Gene Regulation

In the Gray Lab, I contributed to a large-scale mapping effort of the gene regulatory networks (GRNs) controlling the maize phenylpropanoid pathway, a biosynthetic pathway essential for plant structural integrity and defense.

As part of a high-throughput Yeast One-Hybrid (Y1H) pipeline, I identified over 50 transcription factors and uncovered more than 20 novel regulatory interactions. My work specifically focused on validating MYBR87 and DOF3 as key regulators of lignin biosynthesis, contributing to a mechanistic understanding of how transcriptional control shapes crop resilience.
